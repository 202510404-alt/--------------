Lee Kuan Yew, Singapore’s first prime minister, was born on September 16, 1923, and 
is of third-generation ethnic Chinese descent. He excelled in the Senior Cambridge 
examinations in 1940, earning the John Anderson Scholarship to attend Raffles College. 
During the prize-awarding ceremony, he met his future wife, Kwa Geok Choo, the only 
girl in the school. The onset of World War II in Asia disrupted Raffles College, which was 
converted into a medical facility in 1941. After the war, Lee chose not to return to Raffles 
College and pursued higher education in the United Kingdom. Initially, he enrolled at the 
London School of Economics but eventually studied law at Cambridge University. Lee 
Kuan Yew served as prime minister for 31 years, from 1959 to 1990. While his 
government restricted freedoms and maintained strict control over power, he also built 
excellent public housing, transforming Singapore into one of Asia’s wealthiest countries.
