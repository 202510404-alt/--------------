If you suffer from social angst, awkwardness, or dread, know that this is a perfectly 
normal response. No matter what it looks like, a lot of people are dealing with internal 
anxiety in social situations. But it is actually possible to build your social muscle and use 
it to boost your connections — and your anxiety is giving you clues to what icebreakers 
and lifelines other people might be grateful for you to extend. Truly listen to what your 
anxiety is signaling to you about where you feel uncertain or insecure, and practice 
empathy by offering a hand to others who might be in the same boat. Remember that 
flipping anxiety into compassion is something inherent in you — it is, in fact, what your 
anxiety was designed to do for you. Together, compassion and empathy can buffer against 
bad anxiety. You can use your anxiety to be more outward-facing. The outcome? The 
spread of compassion for your fellow human beings, other species, and the planet!
