Emphasizing speed over frequency can make sense in contexts where everyone is 
expected to plan around the timetable, including peak-only commute services and 
very long trips with low demand. In all other contexts, though, it seems to be a 
common motorist’s error. Roads are there all the time, so their speed is the most 
important fact that distinguishes them. But transit is only there if it’s coming soon. 
If you have a car, you can use a road whenever you want and experience its speed. 
But transit has to exist when you need it (span), and it needs to be coming soon 
(frequency). Otherwise, waiting time will wipe out any time savings from a faster 
service. Unless you’re comfortable planning your life around a particular scheduled 
trip, speed is worthless without frequency, so a transit map that screams about speed 
and whispers about frequency may simply be planting confusion.
