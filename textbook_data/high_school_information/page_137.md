Conversely, where human interference prevents elephants from moving freely within 
their habitats and leads to their numbers exceeding the carrying capacity of the 
savanna, their effect can be environmentally disastrous.
One example of human interventions modifying the character of savannas is through 
their effects on savanna-dwelling mammals such as elephants. They are what is known as 
a ‘keystone species’ because they have a strong effect on many aspects of the environment 
in which they live. ( 
① ) They diversify the ecosystems which they occupy and create a 
mosaic of habitats by browsing, trampling, and knocking over bushes and trees. ( ② ) 
They also spread seeds through their eating and defecating habits and maintain or create 
water holes by wallowing. ( 
③ ) All these roles are beneficial to other species. ( ④ ) 
Equally, if humans reduce elephant numbers in a particular piece of savanna, the savanna 
may become less diverse and less open, and its water holes may fill with mud. ( 
⑤ ) This 
will be harmful to other species.
