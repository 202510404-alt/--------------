together and splitting them apart. On the one hand, IT has the potential to increase shared 
information and norms across larger groups. For instance, in the 1960s, most Americans 
got their news from one of three national television networks and a small number of other 
publications. ( 
① ) If Walter Cronkite, a famous American broadcast journalist, said it,

you had liberal friends and interests, you were rarely exposed to conservative news

④ ) In a community where each subgroup saw a different set of

weakened. ( 
⑤ ) In a sense, the United States seemed to be fragmenting into two separate
