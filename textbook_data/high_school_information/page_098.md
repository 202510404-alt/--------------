In music, proximity may refer to elements’ being close together in pitch, time, or 
space. For example, notes that are similar in pitch may be grouped together. 
(A)   Sim ilarly, in some of Bach’s famous solo music for violin, the violinist essentially 
creates two streams of music by simultaneously playing both high and lower notes. 
Perceptually, we group the high notes together and group the low notes together, so 
we hear it as polyphonic or as two lines of music.
