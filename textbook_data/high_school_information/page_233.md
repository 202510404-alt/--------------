Even a seemingly simple protocol such as greeting someone is coded socially. It is 
shaped by appropriate words, phrases, and nonverbal cues that will enable a speaker 
to make successful social contact with another speaker. 
(A)   These frames are coded socially — the y constitute ways of relating to others and to 
oneself. The information exchanged in that one protocol, therefore, goes well beyond 
a greeting. As this example shows in microcosm, social codes derive from traditions 
and are taught via rearing practices.

it reveals the kind of coded behavior that is relevant in the society in which it occurs, 
since there are other societies in which silence or a simple nod of the head may be 
more appropriate as a protocol.
