In academia, you are taught to write up your results thus: “I came up with a 
hypothesis, then I did this experiment, and then I did another experiment, then I tested 
my ideas some more, and so on until [dramatic fanfare] ta-da! I proved this.” 
(A)   Instead, you need to tell us up front: “I pro ved this! And here’s how I know it...” Your 
readership may still dwindle and trail off over the course of your writing, but at least 
the vast majority know what it is you’ve achieved from the very beginning.
